Mitsuki Laycock, more mononymlous known as Mitski, is a Japanese-American singer-songwriter. She was born on September 27, 1990 to an American father and Japanese mother. As she was growing up, she was also moving from country to country on a constant basis due to her father's career. At a young age, she also started exploring and studying music. She first decided to pursue film, before changing to studio composition. She studied studio composition at the State University of New York at Purchase, where she recorded and self-released her first two albums, Lush and Retired from Sad, New Career in Business, which served as her school projects. During her time as SUNY, she also met Patrick Hyland, who will help her in producing all of her albums after Lush.
Her music is known for the depth of the emotions seen in the lyrics and instrumental. It is also known for blending in lots of different genres. Many find her lyrics as "relatable" as Mitski said that her music can serve as a place where people "can put all of their feelings, their ugliness, that doesnâ€™t have a place in their own lives."In 2021, she announced that she will be taking a hiatus from music in order for her to focus on herself, her mental and emotional health.

Lush (2012)

Lush is Mitski's debut album. It was released in January 31, 2012. The album's genre is described to be chamber pop. Mitski wrote, recorded, and released the album while studying at Purchase College's Conservatory of Music. It has 9 tracks, namely:
  1. Liquid Smooth
  2. Wife
  3. Abbey
  4. Brand New City
  5. Eric
  6. Bag of Bones
  7. Door
  8. Pearl Diver
  9. Real Men

Retired from Sad, New Career in Business (2013)

Retired from Sad, New Career in Business is Mitski's sophomore album. It was released in August 1, 2013. The album's genre is described to orchestral pop. This album, just like her first one, is also self-written, self-recorded, and self-produced album. It has 9 tracks, namely:

  2. Square
  3. Strawberry Blond

  6. Shame
  7. Because Dreaming Costs Money, My Dear
  8. Circle
  9. Class of 2013

Bury Me At Makeout Creek (2014)

Bury Me At Makeout Creek is Mitski's third album. It was released on November 11, 2014. The album's genre is described to be a mix of guitar rock, indie rock, folk, and punk rock. This album is quite different to her debut and sophomore because it is more guitar focused, rather than the first two's incorporation of piano and orchestral elements. It has 10 tracks, namely:

  10. Last Words of a Shooting Star
